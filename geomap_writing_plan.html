数据与任务定义

这里要写得非常具体，让 reviewer 一眼明白 benchmark 的输入、输出和人工审核标准。

Input

Article 4 direction 的 planning PDF，来自 Planning Data 中的 document-url 字段。PDF 可能包含扫描页、规划文本、schedule、地图、图例、边界线和 title block。

Output

对应 article-4-direction-area 的 geometry 字段，即 WGS84 Polygon 或 MultiPolygon，可用 GeoJSON 表示。

Human QA

人工检查 PDF 中可见边界和 polygon 是否一致；保留明确对应样本，去除边界不清楚、模糊对应和不对应样本。

字段 论文中怎么写 为什么重要
document-url 模型可见的 PDF 输入。 把 benchmark 定义成 document-to-geometry，而不是只看地图截图。
geometry 目标 polygon，多数为 MultiPolygon WKT/GeoJSON。 提供可计算的 IoU、F1、centroid error。
quality 平台质量字段，如 authoritative。 说明政府平台的来源质量，但不替代人工核验。
QA label keep / unclear / ambiguous / mismatch / removed。 明确 benchmark 只评估可验证的 PDF-polygon pairs。

任务定义 vs. 挑战

任务定义只回答 input/output/metrics；挑战解释为什么这个 input-output mapping 难。

Task

Plan2Map 要求系统做什么

给定一个 UK planning-record PDF D，系统输出一个有效的 WGS84 GeoJSON Polygon 或 MultiPolygon Gpred，表示文档指定的 planning boundary。

  • 输入：PDF 本身，包括文本、地图、图例和页面布局。
  • 输出：可计算、可验证的真实地理坐标 polygon。
  • 评估：Gtrue 只在 evaluation 时使用，用于 IoU、F1、centroid error 和 valid-output rate。
Challenge

间接地理重建

难点不是法律推理，而是 indirect geospatial reconstruction：PDF 里只有分散的文本线索和局部地图，没有目标 WGS84 geometry。系统必须找到相关证据、定位地图、解释边界符号，并输出合法 GeoJSON。

  • 只 OCR：能读出地址，但不能画出 georeferenced polygon。
  • 只分割：能找出图上的边界，但不知道它在英国哪里。
  • 只 geocode：能找到中心点，但不能恢复 boundary shape。
1

Evidence discovery

系统先要判断证据在哪里：notice、schedule、title block、map page、inset map 或单独的 map PDF。这个问题支撑 Reader 和 page/map selection 的必要性。

2

Geospatial grounding

PDF 地图通常没有 WGS84 坐标；系统要把地名、道路、postcode、grid reference 和局部地图锚定到真实英国坐标系。

3

Geometry reconstruction

最终答案不是 mask 或描述，而是 valid Polygon/MultiPolygon；hatching、holes、disconnected regions 和错定位都会变成几何错误。

推荐图注句子：The challenge is indirect geospatial reconstruction: the source PDF contains scattered textual and cartographic evidence, but not the target WGS84 geometry.

六个挑战面：示意图口径

每个 challenge 都要锚定到 PDF 里的具体元素，而不是抽象说“很复杂”。

1

Textual spatial evidence

正文、schedule、title、map labels 提供地址、道路、行政区、排除项和目标边界描述。它们是空间线索，不是法律推理本身。

2

Heterogeneous planning maps

不同 LPA 的地图比例尺、符号、颜色、hatching、透明填充和扫描质量都不同，不能假设统一视觉样式。

3

Implicit geolocation

PDF 通常没有 WGS84 坐标；系统要从 postcode、road name、grid reference、place name、parish 等锚点推断真实位置。

4

Scanned / skewed PDFs

旧 PDF 可能旋转、低分辨率、OCR 噪声强或边界线褪色，导致直接读取和视觉分割都不稳定。

5

Boundary ambiguity

目标边界可能是红线、虚线、斜线填充、多个 disconnected polygons，或被 “excluding No. 10” 之类文本修正。

6

Geometric output

最终答案不是说明文字或 mask，而是有效 WGS84 Polygon/MultiPolygon；任何错定位都会变成几何错误。

相关任务与 baseline 策略

目前没有完全等价的 PDF-to-WGS84-polygon 任务；写法上应把相邻工作分成“可跑 baseline”和“只做 related work”。

Claim

核心定位

Plan2Map 的空白不是“没人做地图”或“没人做文档理解”，而是没有把 planning PDF、地图视觉、隐式地理定位和 GeoJSON 输出放在同一个可验证 benchmark 中。

Use

主文应跑的 baseline

优先跑能直接回答论文论点的 baseline：direct VLM PDF-to-GeoJSON、map-only segmentation、VLM geocode-from-PDF、Collapsed Reader/no dedicated Reader、commit/critic variants。这里 Collapsed Reader 保留 PDFInfo schema，只检验独立 Reader phase 是否有价值。

Avoid

不要硬凑不可比 baseline

MapReader、QGIS/GDAL、UrbanPlanBench、LegalBench 这些更适合放在 related work 或 oracle/semi-manual discussion；不要把它们包装成完整端到端 Plan2Map baseline。

相邻方向 代表工作/工具 和 Plan2Map 的关系 论文里怎么用
Document understanding / DocVQA DocVQA 关注从文档回答问题，说明 multimodal document understanding 的背景；但输出通常是文本答案，不是 GIS polygon。 放 Related Work。强调 Plan2Map 从 document QA 扩展到 document-to-geometry grounding。
Map digitisation / vectorisation Historical map vectorization benchmark；MapReader 覆盖地图图像识别、分割或矢量化的一部分；但通常不从 planning PDF 出发，也不解决文本证据和真实世界定位。 放 Related Work；实验上只和 vanilla/fine-tuned SAM 做 map-boundary segmentation 级别对比。
Classical GIS pipeline QGIS Georeferencer；GDAL polygonize 提供人工/半人工 georeferencing 和 raster-to-vector 的经典工具，但需要人为控制点、清晰栅格或 oracle-like 输入。 作为 classical GIS reference；不要作为端到端 automatic baseline，除非明确标成 semi-manual/oracle。
Remote-sensing / geospatial VLM RSVQA；GeoChat 说明视觉-语言模型已经进入 geospatial imagery，但输入多为遥感图像或地理问答，不是扫描规划 PDF 与 Article 4 边界。 放 Related Work；用来证明 Plan2Map 是文档、地图和 GIS 输出交叉的新设置。
Planning / legal AI UrbanPlanBench；LegalBench 覆盖规划/法律文本理解和推理，但 Plan2Map 的关键不是法律判断，而是从规划记录中恢复可验证地理边界。 放 Related Work；可用一句话澄清“we do not benchmark legal reasoning; we benchmark geospatial grounding from planning records.”
直接多模态模型 baseline Frontier VLM PDF-to-GeoJSON；VLM map-image-to-mask/polygon；VLM geocode-from-PDF 这是最重要的实验对照：如果单个 VLM 能直接从 PDF 输出 polygon，则任务分解没有必要。 作为主实验 baseline。PDF-to-GeoJSON 用 30-50 个 stratified subset 报告为 stress test，不和 full benchmark 完全等价比较。
推荐 Related Work 句式：Prior work studies document VQA, map vectorization, geospatial VLMs, and planning/legal reasoning separately. Plan2Map differs by requiring a system to jointly read a public planning record, identify spatial evidence, ground a local map in real-world coordinates, and emit a valid WGS84 geometry.

向 Paper2Poster 学习的故事结构

Paper2Poster 的节奏是：先定义新任务和 benchmark，再提出 PosterAgent；Plan2Map 也应该这样写。

先定义新任务

像 Paper2Poster 在 3.1 明确 “Given a scientific paper... generate a single-page poster” 一样，Plan2Map 要明确 “Given a planning PDF... output a WGS84 GeoJSON boundary”。

再推出 Plan2Map benchmark

数据不是附录，而是主贡献：公开 planning PDFs、政府 polygon、结构化 metadata、人工审核标签和可复现实验 split。

建立评价体系

像 Paper2Poster 有 Visual Quality、Textual Coherence、VLM-as-Judge、PaperQuiz，Plan2Map 要有 GeoJSON IoU、F1、centroid error 和 valid-output rate，再补诊断指标。

用 direct baselines 制造转折

如果任务只是“看图画边界”，frontier VLM 应该可以直接解决。实验显示它们不够稳定，于是自然引出任务分解。

提出 GeoPlanAgent

像 PosterAgent 是 top-down, visual-in-the-loop，GeoPlanAgent 可以写成 document-grounded, geospatial-tool-in-the-loop：Reader、Locate、Matcher/Segmenter、Commit/Critic。

用诊断分析收尾

强调 bimodal distribution：系统要么几乎完全对，要么完全错。failure analysis、核心 ablation 和可选 oracle-style probes 是这篇文章的成熟度来源。

Plan2Map / GeoPlanAgent writing plan

A benchmark-first narrative for Plan2Map

This writing plan follows the naming hierarchy used by Paper2Poster: Plan2Map is the task, benchmark, and dataset; GeoPlanAgent is the decomposition-based agent for that benchmark, supported by diagnostic experiments for an EMNLP submission.

One-sentence thesis

Planning-record digitisation is a multimodal geospatial reasoning task: systems must convert public planning PDFs into verified WGS84 GeoJSON boundaries.

Four Contributions

Use these four contributions to organize the end of the introduction and the rest of the paper.

C1

Plan2Map: a public, manually reviewed benchmark

We build Plan2Map from paired Article 4 planning PDFs and GeoJSON polygons in UK public-sector planning data, then manually verify that each polygon corresponds to the boundary drawn in the source PDF.

Dataset and Task Definition

Make this concrete so reviewers immediately understand the benchmark input, output, and manual verification standard.

Input

The planning PDF from the document-url field of an Article 4 direction. A PDF may include scanned pages, planning-document text, schedules, maps, legends, boundary marks, and title blocks.

Output

The corresponding geometry field from article-4-direction-area, represented as a WGS84 Polygon or MultiPolygon in GeoJSON.

Human QA

Human annotators check whether the visible boundary in the PDF matches the polygon. Keep clear matches and remove unclear, ambiguous, or mismatched cases.

Field How to describe it Why it matters
document-url The PDF input visible to the model. Defines the benchmark as document-to-geometry, not map-image-only segmentation.
geometry The target polygon, often MultiPolygon WKT or GeoJSON. Supports computable IoU, F1, and centroid-error evaluation.
quality A platform quality field such as authoritative. Describes source status but does not replace manual verification.
QA label keep / unclear / ambiguous / mismatch / removed. Makes explicit that the benchmark evaluates only verifiable PDF-polygon pairs.

Task Definition vs. Challenges

The task definition should specify input, output, and metrics; the challenges should explain why the mapping is hard.

Task

What Plan2Map asks systems to do

Given a UK planning-record PDF D, the system outputs a valid WGS84 GeoJSON Polygon or MultiPolygon Gpred representing the planning boundary specified by the document.

  • Input: the PDF itself, including text, maps, legends, and page layout.
  • Output: a computable, verifiable polygon in real-world coordinates.
  • Evaluation: Gtrue is used only at evaluation time for IoU, F1, centroid error, and valid-output rate.
Challenge

Indirect geospatial reconstruction

The hard part is not legal reasoning. The PDF contains scattered textual clues and local maps, but not the target WGS84 geometry. A system must find the relevant evidence, geolocate the map, interpret boundary symbols, and emit valid GeoJSON.

  • OCR alone can recover addresses, but not a georeferenced polygon.
  • Segmentation alone can find a drawn boundary, but not where it sits in the UK.
  • Geocoding alone can find a centre point, but not the boundary shape.
1

Evidence discovery

The system must first decide where the evidence is: notice, schedule, title block, map page, inset map, or a separate map PDF. This motivates Reader and page/map selection.

2

Geospatial grounding

Planning maps usually lack WGS84 coordinates; the system grounds place names, roads, postcodes, grid references, and local map context in real UK coordinates.

3

Geometry reconstruction

The final answer is not a mask or description but valid Polygon/MultiPolygon geometry; hatching, holes, disconnected regions, and wrong localization all become geometric errors.

Suggested figure caption: The challenge is indirect geospatial reconstruction: the source PDF contains scattered textual and cartographic evidence, but not the target WGS84 geometry.

Six Challenge Surfaces For The Figure

Each challenge should be anchored to a concrete element in the source PDF, not described as generic complexity.

1

Textual spatial evidence

Prose, schedules, titles, and map labels provide addresses, roads, authorities, exclusions, and target-boundary descriptions. They are spatial clues, not legal reasoning itself.

2

Heterogeneous planning maps

Maps vary across LPAs in scale, symbols, colour, hatching, translucent fills, and scan quality, so systems cannot assume one visual style.

3

Implicit geolocation

The PDF usually contains no WGS84 coordinates; systems infer real location from postcodes, road names, grid references, place names, and parishes.

4

Scanned / skewed PDFs

Legacy PDFs may be rotated, low-resolution, OCR-noisy, or faded, making both text extraction and visual segmentation unstable.

5

Boundary ambiguity

The target may be a red edge, dashed line, hatched fill, multiple disconnected polygons, or modified by text such as “excluding No. 10.”

6

Geometric output

The answer is not a description or mask, but a valid WGS84 Polygon/MultiPolygon; any wrong localization becomes a geometric error.

Related Tasks and Baseline Strategy

There is no exact prior PDF-to-WGS84-polygon benchmark; separate runnable baselines from work that belongs in related work.

Claim

Core positioning

The gap is not that nobody studies maps or documents. The gap is the absence of a verifiable benchmark that combines planning PDFs, map vision, implicit geolocation, and GeoJSON boundary output.

Use

Baselines to run in the main paper

Prioritize baselines that directly test the paper’s claims: direct VLM PDF-to-GeoJSON, map-only segmentation, VLM geocode-from-PDF, Collapsed Reader/no dedicated Reader, and commit/critic variants. Collapsed Reader keeps the PDFInfo schema and tests whether the dedicated Reader phase adds value.

Avoid

Do not force incomparable baselines

MapReader, QGIS/GDAL, UrbanPlanBench, and LegalBench are better used as related work or oracle/semi-manual discussion, not as complete end-to-end Plan2Map baselines.

Adjacent area Representative work/tool Relation to Plan2Map How to use it in the paper
Document understanding / DocVQA DocVQA Provides context for multimodal document understanding, but the output is typically a textual answer rather than a GIS polygon. Use in Related Work. Position Plan2Map as moving from document QA to document-to-geometry grounding.

Plan2Map differs by requiring a system to jointly read a public planning record, identify spatial evidence, ground a local map in real-world coordinates, and emit a valid WGS84 geometry.

Storyline Inspired By Paper2Poster

Paper2Poster first defines the task and benchmark, then proposes PosterAgent; Plan2Map should follow the same hierarchy.

Define the new task first

As Paper2Poster states “Given a scientific paper... generate a single-page poster,” Plan2Map should state “Given a planning PDF... output a WGS84 GeoJSON boundary.”

Then introduce the Plan2Map benchmark

The dataset is not appendix material. It is the central contribution: public planning PDFs, government polygons, structured metadata, manual QA labels, and reproducible splits.

Establish the evaluation suite

Just as Paper2Poster uses Visual Quality, Textual Coherence, VLM-as-Judge, and PaperQuiz, Plan2Map should foreground GeoJSON IoU, F1, centroid error, valid-output rate, and diagnostic metrics.

Use direct baselines as the turn

If the task were just visual boundary tracing, frontier VLMs should solve it directly. Their instability motivates decomposition.

Present GeoPlanAgent

As PosterAgent is top-down and visual-in-the-loop, describe GeoPlanAgent as document-grounded and geospatial-tool-in-the-loop: Reader, Locate, Matcher/Segmenter, Commit/Critic.

Close with diagnosis

Emphasize the bimodal distribution: the system is often nearly perfect or completely wrong. Failure analysis, essential ablations, and optional oracle-style probes make the paper feel mature.
